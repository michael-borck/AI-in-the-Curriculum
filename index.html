About This Resource

These materials were developed to support Course Leads and teaching staff in navigating the integration of AI tools into higher education curricula. Rather than prescribing one-size-fits-all solutions, this resource acknowledges the diverse needs across disciplines and encourages thoughtful experimentation.

Core Philosophy

"Not about answers ‚Äì just questions worth asking... together, without shame."

We believe that AI is a tool that can augment teaching and learning when used thoughtfully. The key is transparency, critical evaluation, and maintaining the human elements that make education meaningful.

Key Messages

  • AI is already in use - better to guide than ignore
  • Different disciplines will use AI differently
  • Transparency and disclosure are more important than prohibition
  • AI shame is a barrier to innovation and best practice development
  • Small experiments lead to sustainable change

How to Use These Resources

  • For Presentations: Use the main presentation with the handouts as leave-behinds
  • For Workshops: Combine presentation sections with hands-on activities from the teaching strategies guide
  • For Individual Use: Start with the AI shame guide for self-reflection, then explore practical strategies
  • For Departments: Adapt the discipline-specific prompts and communication templates to your context
